# The skillich Playbook: 6 Steps to Thrive in the Age of AI

This isn't a career ladder. It's a transformation guide. AI is reshaping every knowledge worker role — not eliminating them, but changing what's valuable. The people who understand this shift will define the next decade. Here's how to be one of them.

## Step 1: Find Your Role (2 minutes)

Open the [skillich explorer](https://salahuddinuqaili.github.io/skillich/) and search for your current role. If you're a Product Manager, search "Product Manager." If you're an Accountant, search "Accountant." If you're somewhere between roles, pick the closest match.

What to look for:
- **AI Impact Level** — is your role rated low, moderate, high, or very-high?
- **What's being automated** — these are tasks you should stop doing manually
- **What's being augmented** — these are tasks where AI makes you 10x more effective
- **What remains human** — these are the skills that will define your career

Don't panic if your role is rated "high." That doesn't mean your job disappears. It means the *nature* of your work is shifting. The people who shift with it will be more valuable, not less.

## Step 2: Identify Your Top 3 AI-Resilient Skills (5 minutes)

In your role page, look at the skills table. Skills are sorted by AI resilience — the greenest skills at the top are the ones AI can't easily replace.

Pick the **3 green skills** that:
1. You're genuinely interested in (motivation matters more than optimization)
2. You already have some foundation in (you don't need to start from zero)
3. Are relevant at the level you want to reach next

Write them down. These are your career anchors — the skills that will make you irreplaceable regardless of how AI evolves.

## Step 3: Assess Your Current Level (10 minutes)

For each of your 3 chosen skills, read the proficiency descriptions from junior through principal. Be honest with yourself:
- Which level describes what you **actually do today** (not what you aspire to)?
- Which level describes where you need to be for your next career step?

The gap between those two levels is your growth target. If you're at mid-level in System Design and need to be at senior, that's a specific, measurable goal — not a vague aspiration.

## Step 4: Pick ONE Skill to Deepen This Quarter

Don't try to improve all three at once. Pick the one where:
- The gap between your current and target level is smallest (quick win builds momentum)
- You have opportunities to practice at work (learning without practice doesn't stick)
- The AI impact rating is lowest (biggest long-term career payoff)

This is your **primary investment** for the next 90 days.

### How to practice effectively

- **Read the target proficiency description carefully.** It tells you exactly what "good" looks like at that level. These are observable behaviors — things people can see you doing.
- **Find a project or responsibility at work** that requires the target-level behavior. Volunteer for it.
- **Use AI tools for everything else.** The skills rated 0.6+ in your role? Let AI handle those. That frees your time for deliberate practice on your primary investment.
- **Get feedback from someone at the target level.** Show them your work. Ask what's missing.

## Step 5: Use AI as a Lever, Not a Crutch

Look at the yellow skills in your role — the ones AI is *augmenting*. These are your force multipliers. Learn to:

- **Use AI to do the first 80% faster** — let it draft, summarize, generate, analyze
- **Apply your judgment for the last 20%** — edit, validate, contextualize, decide

The people who thrive aren't the ones who avoid AI or the ones who blindly trust it. They're the ones who know exactly where AI helps and where it doesn't — and that knowledge comes from the taxonomy.

## Step 6: Reassess in 3 Months

Come back to skillich in 90 days. Re-read the proficiency descriptions for your primary skill. Ask yourself:
- Am I demonstrating the behaviors described at my target level?
- What's still missing?
- Has the AI impact landscape shifted? (Check the last_reviewed dates)

Then pick your next skill. Repeat the cycle.

## The Compound Effect

Most people react to AI by either panicking or ignoring it. Both are wrong. The right response is strategic investment: understand what's changing, identify what remains valuable, and deliberately build those skills.

skillich gives you the map. The playbook gives you the route. But you have to walk it.

One skill per quarter. Four skills per year. In two years, you'll have transformed your career while most people are still debating whether AI will take their job.
